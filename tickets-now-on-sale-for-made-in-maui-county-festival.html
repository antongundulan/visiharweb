Tickets now on sale for Made in Maui County Festival | News, Sports, Jobs

The Made in Maui County Festival event grounds at the Maui Arts & Cultural Center are seen from the air. This year’s event is set for Nov. 4-5. Photo courtesy of Maui Chamber of Commerce

The Maui News

Tickets go on sale today for the 9th annual Hawaiian Airlines Made in Maui County Festival, a showcase of locally made products by small island businesses.

This year’s festival will feature more than 140 Maui County vendors offering hundreds of locally made products, including food, art, crafts, jewelry, fashions, home goods and gifts. Live music, product demonstrations, food trucks and entertainment will also be part of the two-day event held in person from Nov. 4-5 at the Maui Arts & Cultural Center, as well as virtually at madeinmauicountyfestival.com.

“We are thrilled to host a hybrid even this year,” Maui Chamber of Commerce President Pamela Tumpap said in a news release Tuesday. “The in-person event is a highlight for the Maui County community attracting thousands of shoppers and adding the online component allows Maui County lovers from all over the world to join in.”

Tickets are $50 for the first day, a buyers preview and exclusive shopping day from 1:30 to 6 p.m. Shoppers will receive a free festival tote bag and complimentary pupu during the opening ceremony.

Tickets cost $7 for the big festival day from 8 a.m. to 4:30 p.m. Nov. 5. The first 2,000 shoppers in the door will receive a free festival tote bag. Shuttle buses are also available.

To purchase tickets, visit mauiarts.org.

For more information, visit madeinmauicountyfestival.com or contact the Maui Chamber of Commerce at office@mauichamber.com or (808) 244-0081.
