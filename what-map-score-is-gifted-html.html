What exactly do the MAP scores indicate?

As soon as students complete their MAP Growth exam, they are given a numerical score known as a RIT score for each area in which they were assessed (reading, language usage, math, or science). When a student receives this score, it shows their current level of performance and may be used to track their academic development over time.

What are RIT scores, exactly?

The term “Rausch Unit” is an acronym for “Rausch Unitation.” The RIT scale is used to evaluate the difficulty and complexity of each MAP assessment question. The scale is divided into five categories. The RIT score of a student represents the level at which the student was answering questions correctly 50 percent of the time throughout the test.

What is the definition of a perfect Nwea score?

On a MAP Growth exam, there is no such thing as a “perfect” or “maximum” result. Despite the fact that the legitimate range for scores is between 100 and 350, this does not imply that student scores “get to 350” or that a student would ever earn the highest RIT item attainable on a test.
