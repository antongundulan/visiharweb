Early Life and Education

Arthur Laffer was born in Ohio, United States. He completed his undergraduate studies at Yale University, where he received a B.A. in Economics. He later earned his MBA and Ph.D. from Stanford University. His academic background laid a solid foundation for his future career as an economist.

Career and Achievements

Laffer’s career began in the government sector, where he served as a consultant to the Secretary of the Treasury during the Nixon Administration. He later served as a member of President Reagan’s Economic Policy Advisory Board. His most significant contribution to economics is the Laffer Curve, which illustrates the relationship between tax rates and tax revenue.

The Laffer Curve

The Laffer Curve is a theoretical representation of the relationship between tax rates and the amount of tax revenue collected by governments. The curve suggests that increasing tax rates beyond a certain point is counter-productive for raising further tax revenue.
