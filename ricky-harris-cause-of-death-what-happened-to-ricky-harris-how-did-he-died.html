Ricky Harris Cause Of Death What Happened To Ricky Harris? How Did He Died?

As the world prepared to bid 2016 farewell, entertainment suffered an irreparable setback: Ricky Harris – known for his iconic roles across both film and TV – passed away unexpectedly at 54, leaving behind fans and colleagues who revered both his work and personality behind. Friends and family confirmed his death. His passing marked an end of an era.

Who Was Ricky Harris?

Ricky Harris quickly made waves in the entertainment world after emerging as an engaging personality from Long Beach, California. Raised as the son of a preacher fathered by spiritual parents – alongside childhood friend Snoop Dogg (later becoming one himself!). Harris stood out among crowd. His distinctive flair set him apart. He spent much time at church choir rehearsals; which provided ample practice time.

Harris’ journey into show business was marked by a blend of humor, character, and authenticity. He navigated the path from his church choir to the Hollywood screen with a natural ease, always carrying with him the charm and wit that would become his trademarks.

The Career and Impact of Ricky Harris

Throughout his career, Ricky Harris amassed an impressive list of credits. His work spanned across various platforms, from the silver screen to television and even into the world of voice acting. Memorable film roles included parts in “Poetic Justice” (1993), where he acted alongside Janet Jackson and Tupac Shakur, and “Dope” (2015), a critically acclaimed modern classic.

On television, Harris first became well known with appearances on “Moesha,” “CSI,” and “ER.” However, his lasting legacy will forever remain his role of Malvo in “Everybody Hates Chris.” Additionally he demonstrated his versatility by joining FX’s series The People V. OJ Simpson: American Crime Story.”

Harris’ talents weren’t confined to acting alone. He made a significant contribution to the hip-hop world, lending his voice to skits on albums by Snoop Dogg and Tha Dogg Pound. His unique voice brought life to characters like DJ Ez Dicc and TaaDow, and he even ventured into video game voice acting with “Grand Theft Auto: San Andreas” in 2004.

What Happened To Ricky Harris?

The entertainment world was left in shock when the news of Ricky Harris’ sudden death emerged. He passed away following a heart attack the day after Christmas in 2016. His manager, Cindy Ambers, confirmed the cause of death, leaving fans and colleagues in mourning.

This unexpected turn of events brought an abrupt end to a career that was marked by laughter, resilience, and talent. Harris’ departure from the world was not just the loss of an actor or comedian but the fading of a unique voice that had resonated across various facets of entertainment.

The Legacy of Ricky Harris

Ricky Harris left behind an immeasurable legacy that extends far beyond screen and stage performance. His memory lives on in his mother, ex-wife, two daughters and the audiences and peers who remember his performances and appreciate their impact.

Condolences from friends and industry colleagues including Snoop Dogg, Ice Cube and Cedric the Entertainer serve to illustrate his remarkable legacy as well as demonstrate just how beloved he was by many in our industry. His death serves as an eyewitness reminder that life can change quickly but that those that leave art or laughter behind leave lasting legacies that leave an impactful legacy behind them.
